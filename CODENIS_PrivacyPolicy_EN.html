3. Information we collect

We do not directly collect information that personally identifies you (such as your real name, home address, email address, or phone number) inside our games. However, we and our third-party partners may automatically collect certain types of information when you use our Services.

3.1 Information collected automatically

When you play our games, some information is automatically collected by the game and by third-party SDKs (such as Google AdMob). This may include:

  • Device information – device model, operating system version, language, system settings, screen resolution, and other technical details.
  • Identifiers – Google Advertising ID (GAID/AAID) or similar advertising identifiers provided by the operating system, for the purpose of displaying and measuring advertisements.
  • Approximate location – coarse location derived from IP address (for example, country or region), used mainly for analytics, security, and to show relevant ads.
  • Usage data – game session length, in-game events (such as levels completed, items unlocked, buttons pressed), crash logs, and general interaction with in-game ads.
  • IP address – used by our partners for security, fraud prevention, and regional settings.

3.2 Information collected via third-party advertising and analytics

Our games may include software development kits (“SDKs”) from third-party partners such as:

  • Google AdMob – for displaying banner ads, interstitial (full-screen) ads, and rewarded video ads.
  • Google Play Services – for basic platform functionality and, where applicable, leaderboards or achievements.
  • Unity / game engine services – for technical game functionality and diagnostics.

These partners may collect and process information under their own privacy policies. We recommend you review their policies as well:

  • Google AdMob & Google Mobile Ads: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads
  • Google Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

4. How we use the information

We and our partners use the information described above for the following purposes:

  • To provide and operate our games – ensuring that the game runs correctly on your device and that core features (such as saving progress, in-game rewards, and difficulty) work as designed.
  • To display advertisements – showing ads, including rewarded video ads (for example, to grant in-game coins or items after watching an ad).
  • To analyze game performance – understanding which features are popular, improving stability, detecting crashes, and optimizing performance.
  • To prevent fraud and abuse – protecting our games and ad partners from fraudulent activity and misuse.
  • To comply with legal obligations – where processing is required by applicable laws and regulations.

5. Advertising and Google AdMob

Our games may show ads provided by Google AdMob and its advertising partners. AdMob may use information such as your device’s advertising ID (AAID), IP address, approximate location, app usage data, and technical device information to:

  • Deliver ads within our games.
  • Measure and improve ad performance.
  • Detect fraudulent or invalid activity.
  • Show personalized or non-personalized ads, depending on your settings and applicable law.

5.1 Personalized vs. non-personalized ads

Depending on your region and settings, Google and its partners may show:

  • Personalized ads – based on your interests, app usage, and other signals associated with your device or advertising ID.
  • Non-personalized ads – based only on context (such as the content of the game) and a limited use of information such as coarse location and basic device data.

Where required by law (for example, under GDPR in the European Economic Area), Google may ask for your consent to show personalized ads. You may also receive only non-personalized ads in some cases, such as when consent is not granted or when child-appropriate treatment is required.

5.2 Google Advertising ID (AAID)

Our games and AdMob use the Google Advertising ID (AAID) to show and measure ads. You can reset or limit the use of the AAID in your device settings:

  • Android: Settings > Google > Ads (or “Privacy” / “Ads”) – here you can reset your advertising ID or limit ad personalization.
